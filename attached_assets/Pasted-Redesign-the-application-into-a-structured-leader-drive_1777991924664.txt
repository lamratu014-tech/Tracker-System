Redesign the application into a structured, leader-driven hierarchical system with controlled access and clear separation of responsibility.

### 1. Core Structure

- The top-level entity must be called “Programme”
- Programme name must be editable by Programme Leads (Admins)

- Under each Programme, create Streams (major functional divisions)
- Under each Stream, create Teams (execution groups)

Final hierarchy:
Programme → Stream → Team → Tasks / Calendar / Files

---

### 2. System Philosophy

This is a leader-driven platform, not a collaborative tool.

- Leaders control all structure, data, and updates  
- Members do not access the system  
- Information flows upward  
- Decisions flow downward  
- Visibility is tiered and restricted  

---

### 3. Roles and Permissions

#### Programme Lead (Admin / Owner merged)
- Full access to entire Programme  
- Can:
  - Edit Programme name  
  - Create, edit, delete Streams  
  - Create, edit, delete Teams  
  - Assign Team Leads  
  - View and edit all data  

Workspace:
- “My Team Workspace” (Global Command View)
- Shows all Streams and Teams with full detail

---

#### Stream-Level Visibility (for structure only)
Streams act as grouped categories (e.g. Marketing, Operations)

- Used to organise Teams  
- Not directly task-managed  
- Visible to all roles within the Programme  

---

#### Team Lead
- Full control of their own Team  
- Can manage:
  - tasks  
  - calendar  
  - members (records only)

Visibility Rules:
- Can see:
  - all Streams  
  - all Team names within those Streams  

- Cannot:
  - access other Teams’ data  
  - edit anything outside their Team  

Workspace:
- “My Team Workspace” (Team-Level View)
- Their Team = full access  
- Other Teams = visible but locked  

---

#### Members (No Access)
- No login  
- No system access  
- Exist only as named records within Teams  
- Can be assigned to tasks  

---

### 4. Admin Deep Access Feature (Important)

Programme Leads must be able to:

- Click into ANY Stream  
- Click into ANY Team  
- Override access restrictions  

This ensures full control when needed.

---

### 5. Visibility & Access Logic

- All users in a Programme can:
  - See Streams  
  - See Team names  

- Only authorised roles can:
  - Open and interact with Team data  

Access Rules:

- Programme Lead → full access to all Streams and Teams  
- Team Lead → full access to own Team only  
- Other Teams → visible but locked  

---

### 6. Dashboard Requirements

#### Programme Lead Dashboard:
- Overview of all Streams  
- Breakdown of Teams within each Stream  
- Progress tracking per Team  
- Overdue task alerts  
- “At Risk” indicators  
- Activity feed across Programme  

---

#### Team Lead Dashboard:
- Their Team task pipeline  
- Calendar  
- Workload overview  
- Stream view showing:
  - other Teams (locked)  
  - status indicators only  

---

### 7. Team UI Requirements

Each Team must include:
- Task management system  
- Calendar  
- Member list (records only)  
- Notes or file section  

---

### 8. Locked Team Preview UI

For Teams without access, display:
- Team name  
- Parent Stream  
- Status (e.g. On Track, Delayed)  
- Progress indicator  

These must:
- Not be clickable for non-authorised users  
- Clearly show restricted access  

---

### 9. Task Workflow

- Tasks are created by Programme Leads or Team Leads  
- Tasks are assigned to Members (non-users)  
- Work is completed outside the system  

Include:
- Manual update capability by Team Leads  
- Optional: simple external update input (no login required)

---

### 10. Calendar

- Controlled by leaders only  
- Filterable by:
  - Programme  
  - Stream  
  - Team  

---

### 11. Design Goal

The system must feel like:
- A command centre for leadership
- Structured, hierarchical, and controlled  
- Clean separation between visibility and access  

Avoid any design patterns that resemble open collaboration tools.

---

Ensure navigation is intuitive and always follows:
Programme → Stream → Team → Tasks
